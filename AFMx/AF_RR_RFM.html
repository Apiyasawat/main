rate control ∽ why
AF is the most common arrhythmic cause of tachycardia induced cardiomyopathy.
Rate control, as well as rhythm control, is shown to reverse the process.
Gopinathannair et al. J Am Coll Cardiol. 2015 Oct 13;66(15):1714-28.
rate control ∽ how
2016 ESC Guidelines for AF
Choice of rate-controlling agents depend on LVEF.
CCB is not recommended in a patient with heart failure or impaired LV function.
The role of digoxin as the first line is limited due to the inefficacy at controlling ventricular rate during exercise.
May use amiodarone for rate control in acute setting; but not in a long-term.
rate control ∽ how - digoxin
Digoxin may increase mortality in AF.
Data from Aristotle showed that in patients with AF currently taking digoxin, the risk of death was independently related to serum digoxin concentration; highest with concentrations ≥1.2 ng/ml.
The risk of death was greatest early after initiation of digoxin in previous nonusers, particularly with respect to sudden cardiac death.
Lopes et al. J Am Coll Cardiol. 2018 Mar 13;71(10):1063-1074.
rate control ∽ how
AV nodal ablation
  • Last resource for those who were UNRESPONSIVE or INTOLERANT to INTENSIVE rate/rhythm control.
  • Remember that the patient will be pacemaker dependent for the rest of his life.
  • Consider CRT; rather than a simple pacemaker, in a patient with impaired LVEF.
  • Sudden cardiac arrest may occur early after AV nodal ablation due to abrupt change in heart rate, sympathetic tone, and K-channel function. Rapid pacing during the first few months reduces the incidence of SCA.
2014 ACC Guideliens for AF and 2016 ESC Guidelines for AF

rhythm control ∽ why
Rhythm control saves life in an emergent situation such as...
AF with WPW, acute myocardial ischemia, acute pulmonary edema, or unstable hemodynamic situation.
rhythm control ∽ why
Rhythm control improves LV function, and cardiovascular outcomes in selected populations.
Ref: CAMERA-MRI and CASTLE-AF
rhythm control ∽ why - vs. rate control
Being in sinus rhythm is the best but how to get there is not easy.
In AFFIRM, rhythm control was not superior to rate control in mortality, CV outcome, or quality of life in an intention-to-treat analysis. On-treatment analysis, however, showed the mortality benefit of being in sinus rhythm was associated with a lower risk of death, while AAD use was associated with increased risk of death.
AFFIRM Investigators. Circulation. 2004;109:1509–1513.
rhythm control ∽ why - vs. rate control
Choosing rhythm or rate control strategy is not a one-way task. You can always change your mind.
For most patients, rate control seems to be the first appropriate step.
In selected cases with highly symptomatic AF or arrhythmia induced cardiomyopathy, early consideration of rhythm control in conjunction with rate control might be indicated.
2014 CCS Guidelines for AF
rhythm control ∽ how
Zoll - Product Manuals
Synchronized Cardioversion
  • Method of choice to restore sinus rhythm in an emergent situation.
  • Anterior-posterior pad position is more effective than antero-lateral position.
  • Unless the patient has been adequately anticoaguated at least 3wks prior, immediate anticoagulation is required before cardioversion.
  • Anticoagulation should be continued for at least 3wks after cardioversion, and lifelong in patients at risk of stroke.
2016 ESC Guidelines for AF and Kirchof et al. Lancet 2002;26:1275-79.

Antiarrhythmic Drugs (AADs)
The choice of the agent depends largely on the presence of structural heart diseases.
Avoid IC agents (Flecainide and Propafenone) in a patient with structural heart disease.

rhythm control ∽ how ∽ AAD - IC
Class IC AADs have minimal to none of beta blocking property.
What that means is...if the drug turn AF into atrial flutter, without beta blocking property, the patient may develop atrial flutter with 1:1 AV conduction. So... when prescribes flecainide or propafenone, remember to add beta blocker to the regimen, unless you really really have good enough reasons not to.
rhythm control ∽ how ∽ AAD - Amiodarone
Amiodarone side effects
The prevalence of adverse effects related to treatment with amiodarone is 15% the first year and 50% in case of prolonged administration.
Higher dose (ie.≥400mg/day) is related to higher incidence of side effects esp. lung toxicity.
Cornea microdeposits (shown in above image) are found in nearly all patients receiving amiodarone for more than 6 months. Unlike optic neuritis, their presence does not require withdrawal of treatment, since they are not related to impairment of visual acuity.
Ref: Merino and Perez de Isla. ESC E-Journal of Cardiology Practice 2011. and Chan and Jhanji. N Engl J Med 2015; 372:1656

Catheter ablation: current recommendation.
Catheter Ablation reduces symptoms, improves quality of life, increases time in sinus rhythm, and increases LVEF in HFrEF when compared to AADs. Additionally, a recent RCT showed mortality benefit of catheter ablation in AF with HFrEF.
2016 ESC Guidelines for AF, Thermocool-AF, RAFFT-2, Meta-Analysis in HFrEF, and CASTLE-AF.

Catheter ablation in persistent AF with idiopathic cardiomyopathy.
The restoration of sinus rhythm with catheter ablation results in significant improvement of LVEF, particularly in the absence of ventricular fibrosis on CMR.
CAMERA-MRI
rhythm control ∽ how ∽ Ablation - CASTLE-AF
CASTLE-AF: the first RCT that showed benefits of catheter ablation on CV outcomes.
Catheter ablation for atrial fibrillation in patients with heart failure was associated with a significantly lower rate of a composite end point of death from any cause or hospitalization for worsening heart failure than was medical therapy.
CASTLE-AF.
risk factors modification ∽ why
Risk Factors Modification (RFM)
Cardiometabolic risk factors such as obesity, physical inactivity, hypertension, DM, and dyslipidemia have all been linked to increased incidence of AF.
AHA Scientific Statement on AF Burden and Lavie et al. JACC 2017;70:2022-35.
risk factors modification
Risk Factors Modification (RFM)
Data from a small RCT and several non-RCTs showed that comprehensive risk factors management is effective in reducing AF burden, improving LA and LV function, and increasing quality of life.
The CENT Study, LEGACY Study, and Circulation. 2016;133:466-473.
latest trial ∽ APAF-CRT
APAF-CRT: 'Ablate and Pace' for permanent AF with heart failure and narrow QRS.
In an elderly patient (mean age=72) with severely symptomatic permanent AF unsuitable or failed AF ablation, narrow QRS, and at least one HF hospitalization in the previous year, AV junction ablation + CRT was superior to pharmacological therapy in reducing HF symptoms and hospitalization.
APAF-CRT.
  • CONCLUSIONS
  • Rate control is an essential part throughout the life of the patient regardless of the treatment strategy.
  • In selected cases with highly symptomatic AF or arrhythmia induced cardiomyopathy, consider rhythm control in conjunction with rate control.
  • Catheter ablation in HFrEF is associated with improved mortality and HF hospitalization.
  • Physical activity and weight control is effective in reducing AF burden.
